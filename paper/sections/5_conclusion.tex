\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We applied a Top-$K$ SAE to the velocity-projection input of $\pi_{0.5}$, a
continuous-time VLA connecting a frozen vision--language backbone to a
flow-matching action expert via a knowledge-insulated interface. The SAE
reconstructs faithfully ($R^2{=}0.987$, $32$-sparse), splits its dictionary
into $2{,}215$ flow-time-stable and $1{,}594$ flow-time-varying features
that qualitatively realise the semantic / motion-phase distinction the
architecture predicts, and produces a steering response $25{\times}$ smaller
than the same model before training---evidence that supervised flow-matching
trains a robust, near-flat continuous vector field along these directions.

\paragraph{Limitations and future work.}
The analysis is correlative; saturation of $\|\Delta a\|$ at large $\alpha$
makes SAE directions empirically indistinguishable from random ones at this
metric. Ablating features under a task-success protocol and hooking the VLM
prefix are natural next steps.
