Sparse autoencoders (SAEs) have proven effective at extracting interpretable
features from discrete language models, but their applicability to
\emph{continuous-time} vision--language--action (VLA) policies is unclear.
We instrument $\pi_{0.5}$, which couples a PaliGemma backbone to a Gemma flow-matching
action expert via a knowledge-insulated interface, with forward hooks at the input
to the velocity projection---the precise boundary at which semantic context becomes
a flow-time-conditioned vector field. Training a Top-$K$ SAE
($d_\text{dict}{=}8192$, $k{=}32$) on activations from $480$ LIBERO frames yields
a faithful sparse decomposition (reconstruction $R^2{=}0.987$, $66\%$ feature usage).
The dictionary splits cleanly into two regimes: $2{,}215$ features
that are stable across the flow-matching denoise trajectory (CV${<}0.5$) and fire
on cross-task semantic concepts, versus $1{,}594$ high-variance features
that track task-specific motion phases. Steering perturbations along learned
feature directions saturate at $\|\Delta a\|{\approx}0.5$ regardless of perturbation
magnitude, in stark contrast to a $25{\times}$ larger and approximately linear response in
the same architecture before training---direct evidence that supervised
flow-matching compresses the semantic-action interface into a robust and
near-flat manifold along structured directions.
