\subsection{Setup}
\label{ssec:exp_setup}
The trained model is \texttt{lerobot/pi05\_libero\_base}; data are $480$
LIBERO-10~\citep{liu2023libero} frames stratified evenly across $6$ episodes
covering $3$ tasks. We log $h^\text{exp}$ on every flow-matching step,
yielding $N{=}48{,}000$ activations of dimension $1024$. Steering experiments
share fixed $(o,\varepsilon)$ across $(\alpha,j)$ pairs.

\subsection{Sparse decomposition is faithful}
\label{ssec:exp_sae}
The SAE reaches $R^2{=}0.987$ after $50$ epochs; $3{,}809/8{,}192$ features
fire on at least $2\%$ of frames, with the remaining $\sim$$54\%$ dormant
under dead-feature reanimation. Reconstruction quality is comparable to SAE
results on LLM residual streams, confirming that the velocity-projection
input admits a sparse code despite living in a continuous-time vector field.

\subsection{Features split into stable and flow-time-varying populations}
\label{ssec:exp_cv}
The median per-feature CV is sharply skewed toward zero (median $0.15$;
Fig.~\ref{fig:fig1}c) with a high-CV tail peaking near $1.5$ and $2.0$;
splitting at $\mathrm{CV}{=}0.5$ yields $2{,}215$ low-CV and $1{,}594$
high-CV features. Their temporal occupancy differs qualitatively
(Fig.~\ref{fig:fig1}d): every high-CV feature in the top-$10$ peak set
occupies a single contiguous band inside one episode (consistent with a
motion phase such as grasp/transport/place), while every low-CV feature
fires as a sparse, episode-spanning speckle (consistent with task-invariant
scene content). Image-space gradient saliency on the top-$5$ frames
(Fig.~\ref{fig:fig2}) sharpens this: feat 2319 localises on the
gripper-on-mug contact region in every task-0 transport frame; feat 6951
lights up an analogous gripper-near-object configuration in cross-task
frames whose backgrounds (white-mug table vs.\ a microwave) otherwise share
no obvious appearance. This is a direct internal signature of the
semantic / physical decoupling the knowledge-insulated interface is
hypothesised to support.

\subsection{Steering perturbations saturate $25\times$ below the random baseline}
\label{ssec:exp_steer}
Fig.~\ref{fig:fig1}a steers along $j\in\{1102,2036,7676\}$ averaged over $4$
LIBERO frames. The trained model gives a $\sim$flat curve at
$\|\Delta a\|{\approx}0.46$--$0.50$ along SAE directions and $0.47$--$0.63$
along norm-matched random directions; the same dictionary directions in an
untrained $\pi_{0.5}$ produce $\|\Delta a\|{\approx}13$ scaling linearly in
$\alpha$, a $25{\times}$ ratio. The trained saturation level ($\sim$$0.5$ in
normalised action units) sits well below the min--max clamp, so the gap is
not an action-bound artefact; we read it as the trained continuous vector
field being locally near-flat along the interface, absorbing moderate
residual-stream perturbations before they reach the action.
